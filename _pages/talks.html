1. Mendelian Randomization Analysis of Multiregional Psychiatric Disorders

(Jun. 2025 ~ Present)
Research Assistant (Supervisor: Dr. Weijie Xie)
Shanghai Mental Health Center, Shanghai, China

Description:

Psychiatric disorders (e.g., depression, anxiety, addiction) represent a significant global health challenge. These conditions frequently co-occur and share underlying neurobiological mechanisms, including neurotransmitter dysregulation and neuroinflammation. While prior studies have identified overlapping genetic risk loci across these disorders, the direct causal pathways linking genetic variants to clinical symptoms remain poorly understood.

This study employs publicly available genome-wide association study (GWAS) datasets to construct a multi-brain region analytical framework. By integrating Mendelian Randomization (MR), Transcriptome-Wide Association Study (TWAS)/Proteome-Wide Association Study (PWAS), and colocalization analysis, we aim to:

  1. Develop a cross-disorder genetic causal map elucidating shared genetic influences on multidimensional psychopathological traits
  2. Identify therapeutic targets with cross-diagnostic potential supported by causal evidence chains

2. Comprehensive Single-cell Analysis of Microglia in Depression

(Aug. 2024 ~ May 2025)
Research Assistant (Supervisor: Dr. Weijie Xie)
Shanghai Mental Health Center, Shanghai, China

Description:

Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) is strongly associated with neuroimmune dysfunction, but the precise mechanisms of microglia-GABAergic neuron communication remain unclear. Using single-nucleus RNA-seq data from MDD patients and healthy controls (GEO database), we:

  • Characterized microglial subtypes and their interactions with GABAergic neurons
  • Prioritized key genes/biomarkers using 69 machine learning models
  • Performed Mendelian Randomization (MR) to assess causality

Key Findings:

  • Identified 3 distinct microglial subtypes
  • Machine learning models achieved moderate diagnostic accuracy (AUC = 0.7–0.8)
  • MR analysis revealed subtype-specific genetic risk/protective factors
  • Disrupted neuroimmune signaling: reduced anti-inflammatory activity and excessive pro-inflammatory responses
