1. Mendelian Randomization Analysis of Multiregional Psychiatric Disorders and Their Associated Symptoms (In progress)
- Jun.2025~Present
- Research assistant (Supervisor: Dr. Weijie Xie), Shanghai Mental Health Center, Shanghai, China
- Description:
Psychiatric disorders (e.g., depression, anxiety, addiction) are a major global health challenge. These conditions often co-occur and share biological roots, such as neurotransmitter issues and inflammation. While studies show shared genetic risks across these disorders, the direct links between genes and symptoms remain unclear.
We are addressing this issue using publicly available genome-wide association study (GWAS) data by combining Mendelian randomization (MR) methods, gene/protein analysis (TWAS/PWAS), and colocalization. Our objectives are two-fold: (1) to develop a cross-disorder genetic causal map that highlights shared genetic influences on diverse psychopathological dimensions; and (2) to prioritize therapeutic targets with cross-diagnostic potential, grounded in causal evidence, to inform precision interventions for mental health conditions.

2. Comprehensive Single-cell Analysis of Microglia in Depression (Completed)
- Aug.2024~May.2025
- Research assistant (Supervisor: Dr. Weijie Xie), Shanghai Mental Health Center, Shanghai, China
- Description:
Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) is strongly linked to neuroimmune dysfunction, but the exact mechanisms of communication between microglia and GABAergic neurons remain unclear. Using single-nucleus RNA-seq data of MDD patients and healthy controls (sourced from the GEO database), we analyzed microglial subtypes and their interactions with GABAergic neurons and prioritized key genes and biomarkers using 69 machine learning models, followed by Mendelian randomization (MR) to assess causality between microglial features and MDD risk.

Three microglial subtypes were identified. Machine learning analysis of two microglia subtypes distinguished MDD patients from healthy controls with moderate accuracy (AUC = 0.7–0.8). MR analysis highlighted subtype-specific genetic risk and protective factors for MDD. Pathway analysis revealed disrupted interactions between microglia and GABAergic neurons, including reduced anti-inflammatory signaling and excessive pro-inflammatory activity.

Our study suggests that impaired neuroimmune signaling—characterized by metabolic imbalances and chronic neuroinflammation driven by abnormal microglia-GABAergic neuron communication—contributes to MDD. These findings identify potential targets for restoring neuroimmune balance in MDD.

3. Immune Mechanisms of the Comorbidity of SLE and MDD (Completed)
- Jul.2023-Jul.2024
- Research assistant (Supervisor: Dr. Jiaoqiong Guan), Shanghai Mental Health Center, Shanghai, China
- Description:
Patients with systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) face a higher risk of major depressive disorder (MDD), yet the shared immune mechanisms underlying comorbidity progression remain elusive. SLE-MDD datasets from GEO underwent WGCNA and differential expression analysis to identify co-expressed modules. A panel of 101 machine learning algorithms was applied to select hub genes and construct diagnostic models, followed by immune infiltration and functional enrichment analyses. The potential biomarker was validated using public single-cell RNA-seq data, immunohistochemistry, and Western blot.

Finally, we propose an immune target that may underlie the shared immune pathogenesis driving comorbidity progression by influencing the function and number of T-cell subtypes. Targeting this pathway may offer novel therapeutic strategies to slow the concurrent progression of both diseases.

4. Microglial Autophagy in Alleviating Depression (In progress)
- Sep.2020-Jul.2023
- Master's Degree Research (Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Wuyang Wang), Xuzhou Medical University, Department of Anesthesiology, Xuzhou, Jiangsu Province, China
- Description:
Major deppressive disorder is a psychiatric condition characterized by depressed mood and anhedonia. Microglia are the primary immune cells in the central nervous system. In response to stimuli, various pro-inflammatory cytokines are released from microglia, leading to neuronal excitotoxicity, synaptic pruning, and demyelination, which impact mood and cognition in individuals. This research focused on the mechanism of microglia in lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced depressive-like behaviors.
